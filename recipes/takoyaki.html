Takoyaki

Back to Recipes

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese street food made of a batter filled with diced or minced octopus, tempura scraps, pickled ginger, and green onions. The batter is cooked in a special molded pan, resulting in round, ball-shaped snacks that are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. They are typically served with takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and aonori (seaweed flakes).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups dashi stock
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup diced octopus
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup tempura scraps
  • 1/4 cup pickled ginger
  • Takoyaki sauce
  • Mayonnaise
  • Bonito flakes
  • Aonori (seaweed flakes)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, dashi stock, and egg until smooth.
  2. Heat a takoyaki pan and lightly oil the molds.
  3. Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about halfway.
  4. Add diced octopus, green onions, tempura scraps, and pickled ginger to each mold.
  5. Fill the molds with more batter until they are nearly full.
  6. Cook for about 3-4 minutes, then use a skewer to turn the balls over, cooking for another 3-4 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Remove the takoyaki from the pan and place them on a plate.
  8. Drizzle with takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise, then sprinkle with bonito flakes and aonori.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!

Notes

Takoyaki can be customized with various fillings and toppings. Common variations include adding cheese, shrimp, or even different sauces. The key to perfect takoyaki is achieving a crispy exterior while keeping the inside soft and flavorful.

Serving Suggestions

Takoyaki is often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer. It pairs well with a cold beer or a refreshing green tea. You can also serve it with a side of pickled vegetables or a simple salad to balance the flavors.
